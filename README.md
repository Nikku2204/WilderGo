# Wilder GO

**Snap the wild, fill your dex.**

Every tree, bird, squirrel, slug and mushroom around you is a creature waiting to be caught. Wilder GO turns the real world into the game: photograph something wild, flick the ball, and hold your breath while the Ranger decides what you have found.

## How it plays

Spot something wild. A street tree counts, so does a weed. Flick the ball when the focus circle is small, and if your aim is true the catch wobbles while the Ranger identifies the exact species, right down to the Latin name. Every catch becomes a collectible card with your photo as the art, a nickname invented for that one sighting, a field entry written on the spot and two abilities true to the species.

Rarity sets the price. A crow pays a little, an orca pays a fortune. New species pay most, repeats pay pocket change, and the third sighting of anything evolves it into a grander form. A fresh quest arrives every morning. Nature Love adds up, and you grow from Seedling to Old Growth.

## Why it is different

Most nature apps run a classifier: photo in, label out. Wilder GO runs a naturalist. The same model that identifies your find also names it, writes its card, invents its abilities, judges your daily quest and pens its field-guide entry, so no two sightings are ever quite alike. The dex is infinite because reality writes it.

## Features

- A one-flick catch with real throw physics, timing bonuses and a proper wobble
- An infinite dex: any identifiable species, sorted into seven guide categories, house pets included as Companions
- Evolutions at three sightings, with species stacks tracking your full history
- Daily quests written fresh each morning and verified straight from your photo
- A field guide for every species, with a verify-on-Wikipedia link and a strict no-foraging-advice rule
- A rarity economy, streaks and seven ranks from Seedling to Old Growth
- A fully synthesised soundscape: birdsong, soft piano and flute that give way to battle music mid-catch
- One file, no install, playable from a link

## Field rules

Look, do not touch. The Ranger never gives edibility, medicinal or handling advice, refuses to identify people, and no catch is worth a dash across traffic. Companions count at common rate; the wild pays better.

## Under the hood

A single React file. Claude Sonnet handles the vision, the judging and every word of prose. Tone.js synthesises the entire soundtrack live, no audio assets exist. Your dex persists between sessions. Mascot art generated with ChatGPT, jungle backdrop with Gemini, everything else built with Claude.
